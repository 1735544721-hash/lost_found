On campus, lost and found items appear frequently in dormitories, teaching buildings, and public areas, and bulletin boards and instant group chats remain the main publishing channels; information is scattered and time-sensitive, making it hard for owners and finders to form timely and effective matches. Current procedures lack unified identity verification and evidence retention, item status, claim records, and notification chains are difficult to trace, and administrators also lack capabilities for global statistics, supervision, and governance. To address these issues, this study designs and implements a campus-oriented lost-and-found system with a front-end/back-end separated architecture, bringing lost-item posting, found-item display, claim applications, and status transitions into a unified business workflow. The front end is built with Vue 3, Vite, and Element Plus to provide category search, time-and-location filtering, detail views, image browsing, and personal-center management, improving information access efficiency and operational consistency. The back end uses Spring Boot 3.2, MyBatis-Plus, Spring Security, JWT, and MySQL to construct modules for user permissions, item information, categories and status, claim applications, notifications, and file resources, supporting image uploads, review routing, and message reminders. While keeping operations concise, the system strengthens data traceability and audit value, providing standardized and manageable service support and an information foundation for campus lost-item management. Keywords: lost and found; campus services; Spring Boot; Vue 3
